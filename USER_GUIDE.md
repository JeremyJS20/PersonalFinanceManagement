# FinOrbit — User Guide

Welcome to **FinOrbit**, your premium portal for personal finance management. This guide is designed to help you and your team master the FinOrbit workflow to achieve total clarity over your financial world.

---

## 1. The FinOrbit Vision
FinOrbit isn't just an expense tracker; it’s a **Net Worth Command Center**. Our goal is to give you a professional-grade view of your "Financial Orbit"—the relationship between what you own (Assets), what you owe (Liabilities), and how your monthly actions (Income vs. Expenses) move the needle.

---

## 2. Navigating the Dashboard
The **Dashboard** is your primary cockpit. It provides three essential real-time insights:
-   **Net Worth Chart**: A visual history of your total balance (Assets minus Liabilities).
-   **Monthly Budget Progress**: A quick glance at how much of your planned budget you've spent this month.
-   **Recent Activity**: The latest transactions across all your accounts.

---

## 3. Organizing Your World

### Accounts
FinOrbit categorizes your money into two main groups:
-   **Banking & Liquid Assets**: Your day-to-day cash, checking, and savings accounts.
-   **Investment & Real Estate**: Long-term wealth like brokerage accounts, crypto, or property.
-   **Liabilities**: Money you owe, such as Credit Cards, Loans, or Mortgages.

> [!TIP]
> You can choose to "Include in Total" for each account. This is useful for tracking accounts you want to monitor but don't want affecting your primary Net Worth calculation.

### Categories
Organize your transactions into **Groups** (e.g., "Housing", "Lifestyle") and **Categories** (e.g., "Rent", "Dining Out"). 
-   **Income Groups**: Money coming in.
-   **Expense Groups**: Money going out.

---

## 4. Managing Transactions
Transactions are the heartbeat of FinOrbit. Every time money moves, it should be recorded here to keep your Net Worth accurate.

### Adding Transactions
When you add a transaction, you'll provide:
-   **Description**: A clear name for the activity (e.g., "Grocery Shopping").
-   **Amount**: The cost or income value.
-   **Date**: When the transaction occurred.
-   **Category**: Which expense or income bucket it belongs to.
-   **Account**: Which account the money came from or went to.

### Filtering & Finding Data
The **Transactions** page allows you to slice your data to find exactly what you're looking for:
-   **Search**: Use the search bar to find specific descriptions.
-   **Date Range**: View activity for a specific week, month, or year.
-   **Category/Account Filters**: See all spending in "Lifestyle" or all activity on a specific "Credit Card."

---

## 5. The Monthly Workflow
FinOrbit is built around a powerful cycle called the **"Cutoff Process."** Following this workflow ensures your data is accurate and permanent.

### Step 1: Daily Tracking
Record your transactions as they happen. Categorize them accurately to feed your charts and budget reports.

### Step 2: Budgeting
At the start of each month, set limits for your expense categories. FinOrbit will track your progress automatically, showing you where you are "in the green" or nearing your limit.

### Step 3: Closing the Period (The Cutoff)
At the end of a financial period (e.g., the end of the month), navigate to the **Reports** section to perform a **Cutoff**.

1.  **Define the Period**: Select the start and end dates (e.g., Jan 1st to Jan 31st).
2.  **Generate Report**: FinOrbit calculates your starting balance, ending balance, and the net flow of money during that time.
3.  **The Lock**: This is the most important step. Once you are satisfied with the report, **Lock it**.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Locking a report** freezes all transactions within those dates. They cannot be edited or deleted, and new transactions cannot be added to that period. This ensures your past financial records remain a "Source of Truth" that cannot be accidentally altered.

### Step 4: Exporting
Need to share your performance with your team or accountant? Every Cutoff Report can be exported as a professional **PDF Report**, featuring high-quality charts and a full transaction breakdown.

## 5. Tips for Success
-   **Be Consistent**: Small, frequent updates are easier than one large end-of-month cleanup.
-   **Use Icons**: Assigning recognizable icons to your accounts and categories makes the dashboard much faster to read at a glance.
-   **Review the Net Worth**: Don't just look at expenses. Watch your Net Worth trend; that's the ultimate measure of your financial health.
